My name is Sota Nakamura. I am twelve years old. I go to Hikari Junior High School. Last month, a new student came to our class. His name is Tom Brown. He is from Australia. He is thirteen years old. He came to Japan with his family because his father works for a company in Nagoya. Tom can speak a little Japanese, but he usually speaks English.

At first, I was nervous because I was not good at speaking English. But our teacher, Mr. Kato, asked me to help Tom. I became Tom's buddy at school. I showed him around the school and taught him some Japanese words. Tom was very friendly and always smiled. Soon, we became good friends.

Tom taught me many things about Australia. He told me that there are many unique animals in Australia, like koalas and kangaroos. He showed me pictures of his house in Sydney. His house has a big garden with a swimming pool. He also told me about his favorite sport, cricket. I had never heard of cricket before, so Tom explained the rules to me. It sounded interesting.

Last week, our class had an international exchange event. Tom gave a speech about Australia in front of the class. He spoke in simple English, and I helped translate some words into Japanese. Many students asked Tom questions. He answered all of them with a big smile. After the event, Tom thanked me and said I was his best friend in Japan.

Now I want to study English harder. I want to visit Australia someday and see Tom's house. Tom is going back to Australia next March. I will miss him very much, but we will stay friends forever. We plan to send emails to each other every week.
