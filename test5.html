My name is Hana Kimura. I am thirteen years old. I am a first-year student at Aoba Junior High School. Every Saturday, I do volunteer work at a community center near my house. I started volunteering six months ago. My older sister, Yui, told me about it. She is sixteen years old and also volunteers there.

At the community center, I help elderly people. I read books to them, play card games with them, and sometimes sing songs together. There are about twenty elderly people who come to the center every week. My favorite person is Mrs. Watanabe. She is eighty-two years old. She always tells me interesting stories about her life. She lived in London for ten years when she was young. Her stories are always exciting.

I also help with a children's reading program on the first Sunday of every month. About fifteen children come to the program. They are between four and seven years old. I read picture books to them in a big room. The children sit on the floor and listen carefully. After reading, we draw pictures about the story together. The children are very cute and energetic.

Last Saturday, we had a special event at the community center. We made mochi together with the elderly people. Mr. Tanaka, who is seventy-five years old, taught us how to make mochi. It was hard work, but very fun. We ate the mochi with soybean powder and sweet red bean paste. Everyone enjoyed the event very much.

Volunteering has taught me many important things. I learned that helping others makes me happy too. I want to continue volunteering and help more people in my community. In the future, I want to become a social worker and help people every day.
